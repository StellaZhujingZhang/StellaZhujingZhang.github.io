About

Welcome to my personal website! I am a PhD student in Civil and Environmental Engineering at École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL), working under the supervision of Prof. Marilyne Andersen. My research focuses on biophilic design, light patterns, and human responses to environmental design.

As both a designer and researcher, I am passionate about understanding how architectural and environmental design elements influence human well-being and behavior. My work explores innovative approaches to creating spaces that enhance human connection with nature through thoughtful integration of light, views, and biophilic elements.

My research particularly investigates Komorebi light patterns - the interplay of light and shadow created by leaves - and their potential benefits for human psychological and physiological responses in built environments.

Projects

Komorebi Light Patterns Research
Biophilic Design | Light Simulation | Human Response | Environmental Psychology
Investigating the psychological and physiological benefits of Komorebi - the Japanese term for the interplay of light and shadow created by leaves. This research explores how these dynamic light patterns can be integrated into architectural design to enhance human well-being and connection with nature.
Research Details Publications
Biophilic Environment Assessment Metrics
Metrics Development | Biophilic Design | Human-Environment Interaction | Assessment Tools
Comprehensive review and development of metrics for assessing human responses to biophilic environments. This work focuses on evaluating the effectiveness of various measurement approaches for views, shading, and interior design elements that connect occupants with natural environments.
Methodology Findings
Spatio-temporal Light Dynamics
Light Simulation | Temporal Analysis | Spatial Patterns | Computational Design
Advanced computational analysis of how light patterns change across space and time in natural and built environments. This project develops new methods for understanding and predicting the dynamic nature of natural light phenomena and their integration into architectural design.
