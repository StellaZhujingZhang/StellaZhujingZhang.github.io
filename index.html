About

I am a PhD candidate in Civil and Environmental Engineering at École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL), working under the supervision of Prof. Marilyne Andersen. My research focuses on the intersection of biophilic design, light patterns, view quality, and human responses to built environments.

My doctoral thesis, "From Komorebi to Shading: View and Light Patterns Through Shading Systems with Spatial and Temporal Dynamics," explores how dynamic light and shadow patterns, combined with view filtering strategies, can enhance human well-being in built environments. I am particularly interested in developing comprehensive metrics for better understanding natural phenomena such as Komorebi, and investigating the design integration of nature-based features that enhance human well-being through empirical study of their relationship to human psychological and physiological responses.

Research Projects

Komorebi Light Patterns & Human Response Research
Biophilic Design | Light Simulation | Human Response | Environmental Psychology | EPFL
Investigating the psychological and physiological benefits of Komorebi - the Japanese term for the interplay of light and shadow created by leaves. This ongoing PhD research explores how these dynamic light patterns can be integrated into architectural design to enhance human well-being and connection with nature through empirical study of their relationship to human responses.
Related Publications Research Details
Biophilic Environment Assessment Metrics
Metrics Development | Biophilic Design | Human-Environment Interaction | Assessment Tools | EPFL
Comprehensive review and development of metrics for assessing human responses to biophilic environments. This work focuses on evaluating the effectiveness of various measurement approaches for views, shading, and interior design elements that connect occupants with natural environments.
Journal Article Methodology
Heat-Resilient Housing Design
Building Performance | Thermal Comfort | Resilient Design | Machine Learning | MIT
Research on mitigating peak load and heat stress under heatwaves through optimization of building systems. This work developed machine learning models as surrogates for physics-based simulations to optimize fan speed and thermostat setpoint adjustments for enhanced thermal comfort and energy efficiency.
Journal Publications Simulation Tools
RoboPinch: Robotic Fabric Formwork
Digital Fabrication | Robotics | Computational Design | Architectural Modeling | University of Michigan
Development of robotic manipulation methods for fabric-formed architectural modeling. This research explored the creation of plaster architectural models through precise robotic distortion of fabric formwork during the casting process, investigating the translative impact of industrial robotics in architectural design.

Komorebi: Embedding Dappled Sunlight in the Built Environment
Master's Thesis | Biophilic Design | Building Systems | Light Patterns | MIT 2020
Master of Architecture thesis exploring how to bring the Japanese concept of Komorebi - dappled sunlight filtered through tree foliage - into built environments. Developed a dynamic daylight-filtering system integrated with solar air collectors to create temporal and improvisational dappled light patterns for buildings with limited access to nature.
